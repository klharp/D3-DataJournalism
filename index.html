2014 American Community Survey Data

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

The American Community Survey (ACS) helps local officials, community leaders, and businesses understand the changes taking place in their communities. It is a premier source for detailed population and housing information. This is a dataset from 2014 with data on state's rates of poverty, age, income, healthcare, obesity, and smoking.

Some Observations
  • States with a higher poverty rate tend to also have a population that has a higher percentage of smokers and those that are obese. The obesity and smoking data clusters in the age range of 30s to mid 40s.
  • The states with the higher percentages lacking healthcare and in poverty are located in the southern and southeastern U.S.
  • The states with the lower percentages lacking healthcare and in poverty are located in the northern and northeastern U.S.
  • The average age for persons all health risks are clustered in the 30s to mid 40s -- the time after being on parent's health plan and before Medicare/Medicaid.
  • There is a correlation between average household income and percent in poverty.
